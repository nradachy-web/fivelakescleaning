Over weeks and months, grease accumulates inside ovens and behind stovetops. Dust settles into baseboards, window tracks, and behind appliances. Soap scum and hard water deposits build up in grout lines and around fixtures. Allergens embed themselves into surfaces no standard mop or cloth ever touches.

Our trained Five Lakes Cleaning teams work through your entire home room by room, surface by surface, using professional-grade eco-friendly products and detailed checklists developed specifically to eliminate the buildup that accumulates over time. When Do You Actually Need a Deep Clean?

Almost every home can benefit from at least one thorough deep clean per year, but certain situations make it urgent rather than optional.

Spring Cleaning Season

Open up your home after a Michigan winter. Clear out months of trapped allergens, dust, and stale air before pollen season hits.

Allergy Season Prep

Dust mites, pet dander, and mold spores compound seasonal allergies. A thorough deep clean dramatically reduces indoor allergen loads.

First-Time Professional Clean

If your home has never had a professional cleaning, start here. Deep cleaning establishes a clean baseline so regular maintenance cleanings actually maintain it.

Before or After Hosting

Holidays, family gatherings, or a big event. Arrive immaculate or recover from a houseful of guests with a complete deep clean.

New Baby or Pet

Introduce a new family member to the healthiest possible environment. Sanitize every surface and remove allergens and bacteria.

Long-Term Rental Turnover

Reset a rental property between tenants. A deep clean ensures the next resident arrives to a truly fresh, sanitized space.

What Our Deep Clean Covers

Every Five Lakes deep cleaning follows a comprehensive room-by-room checklist. Nothing gets skipped. Here's exactly what we tackle, from ceiling to floor, in every area of your home.

Kitchen
  • Clean inside oven, racks, and broiler drawer
  • Degrease stovetop, burners, and hood vent
  • Wipe inside microwave (interior and turntable)
  • Clean refrigerator exterior, coils, and door seals
  • Scrub sink, faucet, and garbage disposal rim
  • Wipe cabinet fronts, handles, and edges
  • Clean countertops and backsplash grout
  • Mop floor including edges and under appliances
Bathrooms
  • Scrub toilet bowl, base, and behind tank
  • Descale faucets, showerhead, and fixtures
  • Scrub tile grout in shower and tub surround
  • Remove soap scum and mineral deposits
  • Clean sink, vanity, and mirror edges
  • Wipe inside and outside cabinet doors
  • Clean exhaust fan cover and light fixture
  • Sanitize and mop floor, including baseboard base
Bedrooms
  • Dust ceiling fans, blades, and light fixtures
  • Wipe all baseboards and door frames
  • Clean window sills, tracks, and blinds
  • Dust furniture tops, sides, and lamp shades
  • Clean behind and under nightstands and dressers
  • Vacuum mattress surface and bed frame
  • Vacuum carpet edges, corners, and under furniture
  • Wipe light switch plates and outlet covers
Living Areas
  • Dust all ceiling corners, crown molding, and vents
  • Clean inside and outside of all windows (interior)
  • Wipe and detail all baseboards throughout
  • Deep vacuum sofas and upholstered furniture
  • Clean TV screens, remotes, and media stands
  • Wipe door handles, light switches, and rails
  • Move furniture to vacuum and mop underneath
  • Clean fireplace surround and mantel
Laundry & Utility
  • Clean washer drum, door seal, and detergent drawer
  • Clear dryer lint trap and wipe drum interior
  • Wipe appliance exteriors and control panels
  • Sweep and mop floor, behind appliances
  • Wipe shelving, cabinets, and storage areas
Throughout the Home
  • Wipe all light switch plates and outlet covers
  • Clean all interior door knobs and handles
  • Dust and wipe all air vents and registers
  • Spot clean walls, scuffs, and fingerprints
  • Clean stair treads, risers, and bannisters
  • Empty all trash and replace liners
  • Vacuum all rugs and mop all hard floors

Regular Cleaning vs. Deep Cleaning: The Real Difference

It's a question we hear often: "We already get regular cleanings, do we really need a deep clean?" The honest answer is yes, and here's why. Regular maintenance cleaning is essential for keeping a home comfortable and presentable on an ongoing basis. It focuses on visible surfaces, routine tasks, and maintaining cleanliness that already exists.

But regular cleaning doesn't touch the inside of your oven. It doesn't scrub grout lines, pull furniture away from walls, clean the inside of window tracks, or tackle the mineral buildup accumulating on your showerhead. Over time, every home develops a layer of deep-seated grime that routine visits simply aren't designed to address. A professional deep clean eliminates that layer entirely and makes your regular cleanings dramatically more effective and longer-lasting.

Many of our clients schedule a deep clean first, then transition to regular bi-weekly or monthly maintenance cleanings at a lower cost. It's the most efficient approach to a genuinely clean home year-round.

Deep Cleaning Questions, Answered.

Everything you need to know before booking your deep clean in Pontiac or Oakland County.

A regular cleaning maintains a home that is already clean, focusing on visible surfaces, floors, and fixtures. A deep cleaning goes dramatically further, targeting areas that accumulate hidden buildup over time: inside appliances, behind furniture, grout lines, baseboards, window tracks, and more. Think of a deep clean as a full system reset rather than routine maintenance. Regular cleanings work best when there's a solid deep clean as the foundation.
We recommend a professional deep cleaning at least twice a year, ideally in early spring and fall. Michigan winters are especially tough on indoor air quality, so a spring deep clean after months of closed windows is particularly valuable. If you have pets, young children, allergy sufferers in the home, or haven't had a professional cleaning in over a year, you may benefit from quarterly deep cleans. Many clients schedule a deep clean first, then switch to regular maintenance cleanings at a lower recurring cost.
A thorough deep clean takes significantly longer than a standard visit, and it should. For an average 3-bedroom home, expect anywhere from 4 to 8 hours depending on square footage, the home's current condition, and the scope of the clean. We send a team rather than a single cleaner to work efficiently without rushing, because the whole point of a deep clean is that no corner gets skipped. We never cut the time short to fit another job.
Very little preparation is needed. We recommend picking up personal items, clothing, and general clutter so our team can access every surface more efficiently, but you don't need to pre-clean anything. That's our job. Do let us know if there are any delicate items, off-limit areas, or specific priorities you'd like us to focus on. We're flexible and happy to customize the clean to your needs.
